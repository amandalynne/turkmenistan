    These are Ireland's top tourist attractions

    	The home of the black stuff saw attendances rise by 18% in 2015, to just under 1.5 million.

        
                Aug 1st 2016, 12:37 PM 
                
                     43,598 Views                 
                
                      52 Comments                
            

              Share124    Tweet   Email2          

        
            THE GUINNESS STOREHOUSE is once again Ireland&#8217;s top tourist attraction.


The home of the black stuff saw attendances rise by 18% in 2015, to just under 1.5 million.


Amongst the nation&#8217;s fee-paying attractions, the Storehouse was followed closely by the Cliffs of Moher Visitor Experience and Dublin Zoo which both registered significant increases in paying visitors during 2015.


The top five fee-paying attractions were:


1. Guinness Storehouse (1,498,124 visitors, +18%)


    Source: Leon Farrell/Photocall Ireland   


2. Cliffs of Moher &#8211; (1,251,574, +16%)


    Source: Eamonn Farrell/Photocall Ireland   


3. Dublin Zoo &#8211; (1,105,005, +2.6%)


    Source: Sam Boal/RollingNews.ie   


4. National Aquatic Centre &#8211; (991,554, -6.5%)


    Source: RollingNews.ie   


5. Book of Kells (767,996, +18%)


    Source: Sam Boal/RollingNews.ie   


Amongst Ireland&#8217;s free to enter attractions, the National Gallery of Ireland once again continues to lead the way with numbers up 21% to over 718,000. Also performing well were the National Botanic Gardens, National Museum of Ireland &#8211; Archaeology on Kildare Street and Farmleigh all making the top five.


The Irish Museum of Modern Art climbed the rankings to third place recording impressive growth of 58%.
